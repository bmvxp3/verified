## What Actually Happens When You Verify an Email

VerifiedEmail doesn't just ping a server and call it a day. Here's what it actually checks, in order:

1. **Scrubs your list first** — removes duplicates and malformed addresses before billing you a single credit
2. **Checks domain health** — identifies domain type, MX records, subdomains, and catches misspellings
3. **Identifies roles and aliases** — flags things like `sales@`, `noreply@`, and forwarding addresses
4. **Verifies deliverability** — distinguishes between permanently dead addresses (deleted accounts) and temporarily unreachable ones (full mailboxes)
5. **Builds a deliverability score** — its proprietary algorithm gives you a score per address, not just a binary valid/invalid answer

That last part matters. A lot of tools hand you a binary yes/no. VerifiedEmail gives you scoring data so you can make smarter decisions — like whether to suppress a "risky" address or send to it anyway depending on your campaign's tolerance.
